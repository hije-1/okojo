## Trigger terms
sanctions evasion, oligarch, elite, export control, dual-use, high-value asset, luxury goods, real estate, private aircraft, yacht, shell company, convertible virtual currency, ruble, kleptocracy, designated person

## Red flags

### Sanctioned-elite asset movement
- RF-RU-1: A newly formed or shell company transacts on behalf of a sanctioned Russian person or entity, obscuring the beneficial owner.
- RF-RU-2: Third-party intermediaries or corporate vehicles purchase high-value assets (real estate, private aircraft, yachts, luxury goods) for a sanctioned oligarch or elite.
- RF-RU-5: A company or account changes ownership or name shortly after a Russian person is designated, while the designated party appears to retain control.
- RF-RU-7: A customer with prior ties to Russian elites suddenly liquidates high-value assets or moves funds to jurisdictions with limited sanctions cooperation.

### Export-control and financial-channel evasion
- RF-RU-3: Transactions involving dual-use or export-controlled goods routed to Russia through third countries.
- RF-RU-6: Payments in rubles or through Russian financial institutions removed from SWIFT, rerouted via non-sanctioned foreign banks.

### Virtual-currency evasion
- RF-RU-4: Convertible virtual currency transfers from wallets or exchanges associated with Russia-based IP addresses following the imposition of sanctions.
