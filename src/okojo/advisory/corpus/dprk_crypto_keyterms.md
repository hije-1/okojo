## Trigger terms
DPRK, North Korea, Lazarus, virtual currency mixer, tumbler, overseas IT worker, overseas laborer, proliferation, ballistic missile, chain-hopping, cross-chain bridge, stolen identity

## Red flags

### Virtual-currency theft and laundering
- RF-DPRK-1: Virtual-currency withdrawals routed through mixers or tumblers shortly after a large exchange or DeFi theft attributed to DPRK actors (e.g., Lazarus Group).
- RF-DPRK-5: Chain-hopping across multiple virtual-currency assets and cross-chain bridges to obscure the origin of stolen funds.
- RF-DPRK-6: Accounts opened with fraudulent or stolen identity documents that transact with DPRK-linked virtual-currency addresses.

### Overseas labor and IT-worker revenue
- RF-DPRK-2: Payments to individuals presenting as freelance IT or software developers whose payment or IP patterns suggest DPRK overseas IT workers using false identities.
- RF-DPRK-3: Remittances from front companies employing North Korean overseas laborers, disguised as legitimate wages.

### Proliferation finance
- RF-DPRK-4: Transactions supporting procurement of dual-use or proliferation-sensitive goods with a North Korea nexus.
