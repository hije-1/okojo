## Red flags

### Illicit oil smuggling and sales
- RF-OIL-1: Shipping documentation inconsistent with maritime-database entries (e.g., a vessel called at an Iranian port, omitted from documents submitted for payment).
- RF-OIL-2: Documents reference vessels with recent or multiple name/flag changes, or ownership transferred after OFAC designation while the designated owner appears to retain an interest.
- RF-OIL-3: Documents reference an IMO number that maritime databases show belongs to a different or scrapped vessel.
- RF-OIL-4: Documents reference "Malaysian blend" oil, especially China-bound via Southeast Asia with AIS irregularities or ship-to-ship transfers.
- RF-OIL-5: A petroleum or shipping company transacts with counterparties or vessels tied to Iran, or that call at Iranian ports.
- RF-OIL-6: Oil-related wires involving vessels linked to prior suspicious activity, or with falsified/omitted bills of lading or consignees, hiding an Iranian nexus.

### Iranian shadow banking networks
- RF-SB-1: Transactions move through multiple exchange houses/trading companies, accruing fees and patterns inconsistent with standard commercial practice.
- RF-SB-2: An exchange house or trading company near Iran uses forged/falsified documents to conceal parties, exploiting correspondent banking to access USD.
- RF-SB-3: Wires or deposits with missing/incomplete source-of-funds information, or that do not match the customer's line of business, especially involving high-risk Iranian-nexus jurisdictions.
- RF-SB-4: A UAE free-trade-zone general trading company with opaque ownership, counterparties in Singapore/Hong Kong, and accounts at multiple UAE banks.
- RF-SB-5: A Hong Kong company on a Chinese non-resident account, little/no web presence, co-located or recently incorporated, sending large unexplained payments to UAE trading companies.

### Iranian weapons procurement networks
- RF-WP-1: Transactions with general trading / suspected front companies with an Iran nexus; opaque ownership, obscure directors, residential/co-located addresses, links to previously sanctioned firms.
- RF-WP-2: Declared business nature inconsistent with transaction history, especially a record of shipments to/from Iran or predominant dealings with technology/chemical suppliers.
- RF-WP-3: Funds directed to companies in unrelated businesses inconsistent with CDD (e.g., receiving from commodities traders but paying electronics suppliers).
- RF-WP-4: Multiple companies incorporated around the same time sharing counterparties, addresses, owners, or name similarities, with little web presence and large recurring transactions.
- RF-WP-5: A Middle East company with Iran links or front-company indicators receiving primarily from petroleum companies and paying primarily to Hong Kong/China electronics companies.
